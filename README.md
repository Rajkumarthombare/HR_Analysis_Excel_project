HR Analytics Dashboard for Employee Attrition & Workforce Insights

🔷 🎯 Problem Statement

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations as it leads to:

Increased hiring costs
Loss of skilled talent
Reduced productivity

👉 The goal of this project is to:

Identify key drivers of attrition
Analyze employee demographics and behavior
Provide data-driven recommendations to improve retention
🔷 🧰 Tools & Skills Used
Excel
Pivot Tables
Pivot Charts
Slicers (Filters)
KPI Cards
Data Analysis
Attrition Analysis
Segmentation (Age, Gender, Role, Education)
Data Visualization
Dashboard Design
Storytelling with Data
🔷 📊 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains HR-related information such as:

Employee demographics (Age, Gender, Marital Status)
Job details (Department, Role, Education)
Attrition status
Job satisfaction ratings
🔷 📈 Key KPIs Tracked
Total Employees
Attrition Count
Attrition Rate (%)
Active Employees
Average Age
Job Satisfaction Rating
🔷 🔍 Approach & Methodology
1️⃣ Data Cleaning

🔷 📊 Key Insights
🔹 Workforce Insights
Total Employees: 1470
Attrition Rate: 16.1%
Average Age: 37

👉 The company faces moderate attrition, requiring strategic HR intervention.

🔹 Attrition Drivers
📉 Age Factor
Highest attrition in 25–34 age group

👉 Early-career employees are more likely to leave due to:

Career growth expectations
Better external opportunities
🎓 Education Factor
Highest attrition among Bachelor’s degree holders

👉 Indicates:

Mid-level employees are most vulnerable
Need for career progression plans
💼 Job Role Factor
Highest attrition in:
Sales Executive
Research Scientist
Laboratory Technician

👉 Suggests:

High-pressure roles
Competitive job market
🏢 Department Factor
R&D contributes 66% of attrition

👉 Critical issue impacting:

Innovation
Long-term growth
❤️ Job Satisfaction
Average rating: 2.6 / 5

👉 Strong indicator of:

Low engagement
High turnover
👤 Marital Status
Single employees show highest attrition

👉 Likely due to:

Higher flexibility to switch jobs
